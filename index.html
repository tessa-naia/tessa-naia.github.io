Lipsync Animation

This animation takes an elderly man’s face inspired by the concept art by Roman Semenenko to animate as it is a more interesting textural and visual basis for lip syncing. Sculpting was used to create organic models and simplifying that sculpt in order to animate.used the fundamentals of lip-syncing animation to a fast-paced, very wacky audio clip by Matthew Mercer audio clip. The texturing was done inside Blender on the model. I believed it was important to keep the sketched lookof the concept art on the beard while still having skin texture bump maps on the face as well as subsurface scattering.

Technologies

Sculpted, textured and animated in Blender

LOW POLY ENVIRONMENT

2018
Collaborative Project with Charles Rabbat.
A 3D modelled environment of a science-fiction futuristic city built inside a mountain made from a stone giant’s head. This model is a fantasy environment which shows the ability of simple low poly environments to deliver a convincing 3D environment. As many environments are designed mainly as a backdrop for a game, it is important to consider that an evocative background is often paramount for setting the scene in any endeavour, be it animation or gaming. Low poly environments carefully drawn can convey much and be the basis from which any other storytelling can spring.
Technologies: Blender, Adobe Photoshop

Monster

This organic model showcases my skill in realistic texturing and rigging. This creature’s textures are a combination of frogs, bats, deer, rhinos and geckos, blended together using texture, specular and bump maps. The model was rigged in Blender with a fly cycle with attention to realism and with extra animations like blinking to give the model even more life. This model was lit using environment lighting and an HDR image so that you can see the reflection of the environment in the eye as well as the light bounce off the monster’s skin especially under the wings.

Technologies

Blender, Adobe Photoshop

World Artificer

This world building web tool allows the user to explore consequences of their world building choices. It seeks to inspire the user to see the interconnected consequences of certain aspects of their design and thus produce more robust and out of the box settings. It includes three main pages. Including the user’s galaxy, where they can view their database of planets, the explore page where the user can view worlds created by others and the design page in which the user can customize a planet and have those options appear visually.

Technologies

The front-end was built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Backend connected using PHP and a MySQL database. Assets were made in Adobe Illustrator or hand drawn in Adobe Photoshop.

FOUND IN TIME - 2D ANIMATION

Self-Directed 2D film animation project inspired by the theme found in time. I aimed to create a feeling of nostalgia by hand drawing the walk and fly cycle of the paper bag character as well as creating the backgrounds out of craft paper and adding textured layers to give the illusion of age. The sound was designed to add to the nostalgic effect with a gentle jazz tune and clips of crumpling paper, leaves and footsteps.

Technologies

Adobe After Effects, Premiere and Photoshop

HackConcordia | Director of Design

Client Work for the HackConcordia association in collaboration with Abby Schafer on the Design team and the rest of the HackConcordia Executive Team.
As Director of Design on the HackConcordia Executive team I was responsible for design, branding and the smooth organization of Concordia’s hackathon ConUHacks. The organization had two social media presences; we developed a sleeker design for the HackConcordia social media that could be carried on in future years. Whereas the ConUHacks social media followed the retro gaming design we developed for the event. Worked with the tech team to create assets for the event website following the 8-bit theme of the event. I worked to coordinate the look and feel of the overall project which required constant communication between the various people coordinating design. Designed pamphlets, event lanyards and swag items for participants and sponsors such as t-shirts and enamel pins.
